Plastic Waste in European countries

The aim of this analysis would be to provide a comparative study of plastic waste in European different countries, focusing on answering the following research questions:

"What is the relationship between the plastic waste generated and that treated? Which countries in Europe are making the greatest progress in reducing their plastic waste and how is each country treating this waste?"

The gap between plastic waste generated and plastic waste treated keeps increasing in Europe

In 2020 there is a 70% increase from the difference in 2010, European countries are not keeping the pace of their plastic waste generated.
Hover on a line to display the gap between generated and treated

Here we present the relationship and trend between plastic waste generated and plastic waste treated in Europe. A line chart shows how the amount (in tonnes) of plastic waste generated and plastic waste treated evolved across the years. Both variables have a positive trend, so we focus on the gap between the two (grey line). This clearly shows that the generation of plastic is growing gaster than what European countries are able to treat.

How many kilograms (kg) of plastic waste do you generate on average in one year?

Belgium, Italy, Luxembourg and Austria generate over 40 kg of plastic waste in a year. Montenegro, Türkiye and Serbia lead the plastic environmental progress with less than 10 kg of plastic waste per capita.
Hover on a bar to display its value or on the line to display the mean

This bar chart shows the amount of plasic waste generated per capita on average (calculated from 2010 to 2020) in a year in European countries. The aim of this visualisation is to be able to compare how much plastic waste is being generated by citizens in different countries, in particular which are generating more or less plastic waste compared to the European average.

Some smaller countries that do not add much plastic waste to the absolute value generated, have a large ratio per capita, which represents what a citizen generates on average. This is the case of Luxembourg and Belgium.

The amount of plastic waste treated does not reach the total amount generated in Europe, with only one exception: Türkiye

Türkiye not only treats its total amount of plastic waste generated, but also receives and treats almost three times this figure, serving as a landfill for other European countries.
Hover on a bar to display its value

This grouped bar chart shows the total amount of plastic waste generated and treated (in tonnes) from 2010 to 2020 across European countries. It aims to compare the impact of plastic waste generated in each country in absolute values, as well as how much of this quantity is being treated.

Italy, Germany, the UK, France and Poland account for most part of the plastic waste generation in Europe in the past 10 years. According to the previous bar plot, Italy and the UK still represent some of the highest values per capita.

The percentage of plastic waste treated over generated varies greatly among European countries. The UK, Belgium, Greece, Ireland and Estonia are the least accountable countries for the plastic the produce.

How is your country treating its plastic waste?

Although treating the total amount of plastic waste generated should be a priority, management operations have different impact on the environment. In particular, recovery (recycling, energy recovery and backfilling) is greener than disposal (incineration and disposal in landfills).
